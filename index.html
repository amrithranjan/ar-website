I always got to witness the joy of Western holidays through my friends and neighbors. Begrudgingly I thought we would never have any fun. But, when I lost my first tooth, my mom introduced me to the Tooth Fairy. Finally a little magic.

Looking back, I realize my mom used the Tooth Fairy as a manipulative strategy; I can still hear her saying, “If you don’t brush your teeth, the Tooth Fairy will never want them.” So, every morning and night, my twin sister and I would competitively brush our teeth together to ensure that the Tooth Fairy would not overlook us. I would love going to the dentist so I could excitedly exclaim that five more dollars had appeared from another lost tooth. I believed in my endless supply of teeth that would always garner me a fortune. But, I soon learned my fate: only a few baby teeth remained. Tooth Fairy visits were nearing an end.

When I was thirteen years old, the Tooth Fairy sadly said goodbye. I’m not sure if it was my mom’s emphasis on brushing or a little magic, but a growing interest in dentistry had started to bloom.

I was able to have the opportunity to shadow for over 100 hours, learning about different procedures and how a dental practice is run. I observed dental procedures and practice’s operations, prepped patients for appointments, assisted in taking x-rays, and made goody bags filled with toothbrushes, toothpaste, mouthwash, and floss.

From a young age, dentistry seemed to be the right career for me. Additionally, through research and hands-on practice, it became more apparent. My skills at crocheting and playing the flute helped me work with both hands and the dental tools. Experience at blood drives and vaccination centers helped me learn how to work with patients. Even the annual dentist visits as a kid were something I looked forward to, despite being many children's fear.

However, I truly found my passion for dentistry when I interned later at a general practice, Smiles at Sunrise.

Working at a general practice helped me realize that dentistry is more than pretty smiles and telling kids to floss. In particular, during a three hour appointment, one patient explained how growing up in Rockbridge County there were no dental practices near him, causing him to need extensive procedures for his teeth when older. Somehow, between creating goody bags for kids and watching cavities get filled, I had forgotten that many people did not grow up in luxury.

Through diversifying my dental experience, I recognized the need for dental care in impoverished areas. The passion I was searching for was not in the career but the location I chose, for the patient and a generation to come. While my interest in dentistry started as a kid appreciating five dollars under the pillow, I hope my passion can help share the magic of the Tooth Fairy for generations to come.
